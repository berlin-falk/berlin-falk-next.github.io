CC STUDIO

For everyone

CC Studio is CC’s learning centre. We’re based on the first floor with workshops, exhibition spaces, and room for immersion, learning, and relaxation. We arrange guided tours, workshops, and art talks for children, young people, and adults and invite them to take part in workshop activities and attend lots of special events during the year.

We want to develop learning activities that will inspire you to experience art on your own and with others. Please contact us if you have comments or suggestions for collaborative ventures or new activities.

Guided tours for children every weekend

On Saturdays and Sundays at 14.00, art educators invite children along with their adults to attend children’s guided tours of the exhibitions. The tour lasts about 30 minutes and is free when the entrance fee has been paid. Together, we examine the art through dialogue, play, and activities.

Recommended age: 4–12 years.

Children’s birthday parties at CC

Birthday celebrations with creative activities, play, and a tour of the exhibitions at CC.
After activities guided by our art educators, you can continue your party in a separate room. You bring your own food and drink and clear up afterwards. The children must be under adult supervision for the duration of the visit. We expect the adults who bring the children to take part and lend a hand with the practicalities, ensuring that all children will have a positive experience. Admission fees are charged for extra adults.

We offer two different formats:

1: Children’s birthday party with a creative activity together with an art educator (about 45 minutes) plus 1 hour in the room on your own.

Price: DKK 1000.

Max. 20 children and 3 adults.

2: Children’s birthday party with a guided tour for children (about 45 minutes), creative activity (about 45 minutes) plus 1 hour in the room on your own.

Price: DKK 1750.

Max. 20 children and 3 adults.

A two-hour birthday party is bookable for Saturdays and Sundays from 11.15–13.15; 12.30–14.30; or 14.30–16.30.

If you want a three-hour birthday party, we’ll find a time that suits everyone.

Private events at CC during the weekend

Our art educator will give you a tour of the exhibitions and afterwards you’re free to spend 1 hour in a separate room at CC. You clear up afterwards. If there are children present, they must be under adult supervision for the duration of your visit.

We offer two different formats:

Learning

CC Studio invites you to join playful and instructive collective activities based on art. CC Studio is CC’s learning and communication initiative, setting out various creative trails throughout the art centre.

Primary school

Pictorial analysis

Topics: Art history, pictorial analysis

Subjects: Danish, visual art, history

Which methods and tools can we use when looking at art? And how do we tackle contemporary art? This programme takes us round the various exhibitions, prises open the works with questions and activities, and tests various methods of artwork analysis.

We adapt all our programmes to the age group visiting us. Our art educator would like to know if there’s something particular, you want to focus on.

Workshop: In our two-hour programme, pupils will have an opportunity to work practically and creatively with pictorial analysis in CC Studio’s workshop.

Contemporary art

Topics: Contemporary art, perspective, collaboration, analysis, participation

Subjects: Danish, visual art, history

What is contemporary art? How do we define and talk about it? We examine, analyse, and put perspective on contemporary art at CC and try to learn more about the times we live in.

We adapt all our programmes to the age group visiting us. Our art educator would like to know if there’s something particular, you want to focus on.

Workshop: In our two-hour programme, pupils will have an opportunity to work practically and creatively with contemporary art in CC Studio’s workshop.

Performance scripts

Topics: Performance art, contemporary art

Subjects: Danish, visual art, drama

What is performance art and how does a performance artist work? In this programme, pupils will learn about art by creating it themselves. We look at CC’s archive of performance scripts accumulated and extended on a continuous basis in conjunction with international artists. In art history, scripts for performance art are called event scores and have been used by many artists as a means to making their live works more accessible and unconstrained by time and space. With an event score, anyone can stage their own performance as and when it suits them. The programme gives pupils an opportunity to test the event scores of others and produce their own.

We adapt all our programmes to the age group visiting us. Our art educator would like to know if there’s something particular, you want to focus on.

Workshop: In our two-hour programme, pupils will have an opportunity to work practically and creatively with performance art in CC Studio’s workshop, in the exhibitions, and in and outside the building.

Art using all our senses

Topics: Senses, colours, play, motoric function

Subjects: Visual art, Danish, craft and design

This programme examines the exhibitions at CC using every human faculty. We move through the various exhibitions, involve the faculties of hearing, smell, and touch. We challenge the body, play, and discuss what we experience as we go. What happens when viewers are transformed into participants? And how do we experience art when we’re active in it and with it?

Workshop: In our two-hour programme, pupils will have an opportunity to work practically and creatively by involving several faculties and materials in CC Studio’s workshop.

Art as a space for play and learning

Topics: Art education, dialogue teaching, user involvement

CC Studio is CC’s learning centre. This programme gives you an introduction to dialogue learning and the various forms of art education which underlie our work. At CC, play, the body, and sensation are always integral to encountering art and with our art educator, you have an opportunity to try out the various exercises we have developed. The programme invites exploration of the special reflective space that art can help create.

An art institution without a collection

Topics: Art institutions, curating, institutional analysis, cultural heritage

Copenhagen Contemporary is an art centre and not a museum. But what’s the difference? And how is an art centre run? Which challenges and special possibilities do we have at CC? Our art educator will take you through the centre and examine exhibitions, activities and spaces. You will get an idea of CC as a cultural institution and an introduction to organising exhibitions, education, events, etc.

About CC Studio

CC Studio is synonymous with CC’s strategy for presentation and learning. CC Studio is both a physical location at CC and a term defining the way we think and practise learning throughout the art centre. We use the word ’studio’ because it means ’workshop’ and refers to a place intended for study and reflection where materials can be examined and issues explored further. CC Studio is based on the first floor at CC, complete with a creative workshop, exhibition spaces, and space for immersion, learning, and relaxation. CC Studio is centred on collective activities.

At CC, we organise exhibitions to be experienced with your whole body and from which you will hopefully gain inspiration. Art which might prompt questions, get you talking, thinking, laughing, playing, discussing, and getting to know others.

CC Studio designs educational materials for everyone to use as inspiration during their visit to the art centre. We put great store by dialogue and the exchange of experiences and ideas. Our exhibition hosts are always happy to chat with you about art. Just ask them.

Our facilities, materials, and activities are designed to allow children, young people, and adults to be together. If you’re not used to visiting exhibition venues, we’d be happy to show you some ideas on how, together, you can experience the centre and its art. Our goal is that everyone gets an opportunity to experience art in an engaging and informal way.

We encourage you to examine and experience the art in all manner of ways – on your own, together with someone you know, or with those you meet at the art centre. New social set-ups are formed and existing ones strengthened when we come together through creative and physical activities in the exhibitions. A fact we consider important.

Guided tours for children

On Saturdays and Sundays at 14.00, art educators invite children and their accompanying adults to attend guided tours for children through the exhibitions where they can examine and experience art. Focus is directed at examining the art using all faculties. The guided tour always takes its point of departure in what the children find interesting.

Workshop

The workshop is open on Saturdays and Sundays from 11.00–17.30 where children and adults can work with different materials together with our workshop artists. Working in the workshop is free when the admission fee has been paid. You can also book the workshop on weekdays.

Temporary exhibition in hall 4

Each year, we invite an acclaimed international artist to create a site-specific total installation for CC Studio – a so-called playscape which everyone can explore, play in, and examine using their bodies. The Swedish artist Jacob Dahlgren has created our first playscape, entitled Art is Life and you can experience it in hall 4 on the first floor.

Performance, Art Bar, Culture Night, and much more

In 2020, we place special focus on performance for children and young people via performance workshops, a series of performances, and separate workshops. We take part in the Culture Night event and invite you to attend the Art Bar event where children show the art and their favourite spots in the art centre to each other.

Schools and institutions

Art can be a unique starting point for learning and frame the context for activities, play, and dialogue, building the foundations for the formation that will ultimately turn us into active citizens. We have, therefore, designed learning programmes and courses for institutions, young families, upper secondary education, workplaces, and other groups. Both as permanent programmes, such as pictorial analysis and contemporary art, which you can book at any time –and special programmes based on current exhibitions.

We use dialogue-based teaching for all age groups – adults, too. Schools and institutions are welcome to visit CC on their own and can also book educational programmes. Write to us at ccstudio@cphco.org or call +45 2989 8087.

CC Studio designs projects in collaboration with housing associations, institutions, festivals, and many others

Together with numerous other people, we are continuously developing new ways in which to work with art, community spirit, life changes, and democracy. Centred on art, we work closely with diverse groups of children, youngsters, and adults from many walks of life in meaningful and stimulating collective action to create change and encourage a sense of community.
We take part with projects and agenda items at the Folkemødet (a Danish festival for democracy and dialogue), the Folkemøde for Young People, Roskilde Festival, and the cultural event on the island of Mors, Kulturmødet.
We want to make a difference to society by helping to ensure that everyone has equal opportunities to take part in, experience, and access social and cultural resources.
We realise that art can be a powerful platform for motivating people and stimulate ideas to take an active part in communal life. Under the heading Fællesskabsprojekterne (Community activity projects), we want to work to empower mixed community groups in society.

You are most welcome to contact us with your enquiries, ideas, and suggestions for collaborative projects. Follow CC Studio’s initiatives and activities in Journal and in the calendar.
